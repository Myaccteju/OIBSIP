  • 19th February, 1630

    Birth of a Warrior

    Shivaji Maharaj was born in the hill fort of Shivneri near Junnar, Maharashtra. From an early age, he was inspired by the stories of great Indian warriors. His mother Jijabai played a significant role in instilling patriotic and spiritual values in him.

  • 1643-47

    Conquest of the Hill Forts of Kondana, Torna and Raigad

    Young Shivaji began asserting his leadership by capturing forts like Kondana, Torna, and Raigad. These early victories laid the foundation for his independent kingdom. His actions demonstrated military genius and vision beyond his years.

  • 7th March, 1647

    The Loss of a Mentor – Dadaji Kondadev's Demise

    Dadaji Kondadev, Shivaji's guardian and administrator, passed away. His death gave Shivaji full control over his father's jagir. Shivaji began to strengthen his political and military hold with greater autonomy.

  • April-May, 1656

    Victory Over Javli – The Turning Point

    Shivaji captured Javli from the Maratha noble Chandra Rao More. This victory increased his territorial control and gave him access to more resources. It marked a critical step toward building the Maratha Empire.

  • March-May, 1657

    First Clash with the Mughals – The Ahmednagar Raids

    Shivaji’s early skirmishes with the Mughal Empire began with his raids into the Ahmednagar region. This was the beginning of long-standing conflict between the Marathas and the Mughals. It also attracted Mughal attention toward his rising power.

  • 10th November, 1659

    The Fall of Afzal Khan – Triumph Over Bijapur

    Bijapur's general Afzal Khan tried to eliminate Shivaji through treachery. In a dramatic encounter, Shivaji killed Afzal Khan using his concealed weapon, the "wagh nakh" (tiger claws). This victory boosted Maratha morale and reputation.

  • January 1660

    Pune Under Siege – Shaista Khan's Occupation

    Shaista Khan, a powerful Mughal general, took control of Pune. He posed a serious threat to Shivaji's domain. His presence in the region created tensions and set the stage for future retaliation by Shivaji.

  • 5th April, 1663

    The Daring Palace Raid – Shaista Khan Attacked

    In a bold night raid, Shivaji attacked the Lal Mahal in Pune, where Shaista Khan resided. Shivaji wounded the general and escaped safely. This fearless move humiliated the Mughals and elevated Shivaji's status as a master strategist.

  • January, 1664

    Plunder of Surat – A Strategic Blow

    Shivaji raided the prosperous Mughal port city of Surat. He looted immense wealth, disrupting Mughal trade. The raid demonstrated his ability to strike economic blows in addition to military ones.

  • 11th June,1665

    Treaty of Purandar – Tactical Diplomacy

    After being besieged by Raja Jai Singh, Shivaji was compelled to sign the Treaty of Purandar. He agreed to surrender several forts but used this as a tactical pause. The treaty revealed his flexibility in diplomacy when needed.

  • 17th August, 1666

    The Great Escape from Agra

    Shivaji was held in house arrest by Emperor Aurangzeb in Agra. Using clever disguise and planning, he managed a daring escape with his son Sambhaji. The incident became legendary in Indian history.

  • 3-4 October, 1670

    Surat Struck Again – Shivaji’s Vengeance

    Shivaji raided Surat a second time, reaffirming his control over western trade routes. It was also an act of retaliation against Mughal treachery. The loot funded further expansion of the Maratha state.

  • 6th June, 1674

    The Coronation – Rise of Chhatrapati

    Shivaji was formally crowned as Chhatrapati at Raigad Fort. The grand ceremony marked the birth of Hindavi Swarajya (self-rule). He assumed a sovereign identity and established formal governance systems.

  • 1676-1677

    Southern Expedition – Conquest of Jinji and Vellore

    Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj led his final military campaign into southern India, capturing forts like Jinji and Vellore. These conquests extended Maratha influence deep into the south. It reflected his ambition to unify and strengthen his kingdom.

  • 3rd April, 1680

    End of an Era – Shivaji’s Demise

    Shivaji Maharaj passed away in 1680 at Raigad Fort. His death marked the end of an extraordinary era but the Maratha Empire he built continued to grow. His ideals and courage left a lasting legacy in Indian history.

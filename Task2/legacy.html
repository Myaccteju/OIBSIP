FORT: HARISHCHANDRAGAD
Location: Ahmednagar, Maharashtra
Nestled amidst the Sahyadri mountains of Maharashtra, India, Harishchandragad stands as a majestic testament to history and nature’s grandeur. This ancient hill fort, towering over the landscape at an elevation of 4,670 feet, has captivated the hearts of adventurers and history enthusiasts for centuries.
FORT: JINJI
LOCATION: TAMIL NADU
Jinji Fort, also known as Gingee Fort, is a historical fortress located in the Tamil Nadu state of India. It is strategically situated on three hills and is considered a significant landmark due to its historical and architectural importance. The fort is known for its formidable structure and the strategic location it offered, making it a coveted prize for various rulers throughout history
FORT: KHANDERI
LOCATION: Alibaug, Maharashtra
Khanderi Fort, also known as Angre Island, is a historical fort located on a small island near Alibaug, Maharashtra. It was a significant stronghold of the Maratha Navy under Kanhoji Angre, who strategically used it to challenge the Siddis of Janjira. The fort's importance lies in its role as a naval base and its strategic location, serving as a defensive structure and a vantage point for monitoring and controlling naval activities.
FORT: LOHAGAD
LOCATION: Pune, Maharashtra
Lohagad Fort is a majestic hill fort situated in the Sahyadri hills of Maharashtra, India. It is located near the hill station of Lonavala and stands at an impressive elevation of 1,033 meters (3,389 feet) above sea level. The fort’s name, Lohagad, translates to “Iron Fort,” alluding to its impenetrable and sturdy construction.
FORT: MURUD-JANJIRA
LOCATION: Raigad, Maharashtra
Murud-Janjira Fort, also known simply as Janjira Fort, is a majestic island fortress situated off the coastal town of Murud in the Raigad district of Maharashtra, India. Renowned for its unique architectural design and unwavering resilience, Janjira Fort has proudly stood the test of time, earning its reputation as one of India’s most formidable coastal fortifications
FORT: PANHALA
LOCATION: Kolhapur, Maharashtra
Panhala Fort (also known as Panhalgad and Panhalla, literally “the home of serpents”) is a majestic hill fort situated in Panhala, 20 kilometers northwest of Kolhapur in Maharashtra, India. It stands tall, strategically positioned overlooking a crucial pass in the Sahyadri mountain range, which served as a vital trade route connecting Bijapur in the interior of Maharashtra to the coastal regions. This strategic location made Panhala Fort the center of numerous skirmishes in the Deccan Plateau, involving the Marathas, the Mughals, and the British East India Company.
FORT: PRATAPGAD
LOCATION: Mahabaleshwar, Maharashtra
Pratapgad Fort is a mountain fort located in Satara district, in the Western Indian state of Maharashtra. The fort is situated 24 kilometres (15 mi) from the Mahabaleshwar hill station. The fort is now a popular tourist destination.

Raigad is one of the historically significant forts in Maharashtra. Nestled amid the picturesque Sahyadri mountain ranges in Maharashtra, the Raigarh Fort stands tall as a symbol of historical grandeur and architectural brilliance. FORT: RAJGAD
LOCATION: Pune, Maharashtra
Rajgad Fort, located in Maharashtra, India, is a majestic hill fortress with a history tracing back to the 17th century. Originally known as Murumdev, it was later captured and expanded by the Maratha king Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, who made it his capital. The fort's strategic position atop the Sahyadri mountains and its impregnable defenses made it a symbol of Maratha power and resistance against invaders. Rajgad Fort stands as a significant historical landmark, representing the valor and strategic acumen of the Maratha Empire during its reign.
FORT: SALHER
LOCATION: Nashik,Maharashtra
Salher Fort, situated in Maharashtra, India, is a significant hill fort with historical origins dating back to the 10th century. It was initially built by the Rashtrakutas and later came under the control of various dynasties, including the Marathas. Salher Fort's strategic location atop the Sahyadri mountain range made it a crucial defensive outpost during different periods of history. The fort's architecture showcases a blend of various cultural influences. Today, it stands as a popular trekking destination, attracting adventurers and history enthusiasts alike who seek to explore its intriguing past and admire the surrounding natural beauty.
FORT: SHIVNERI
LOCATION: Pune, Maharashtra
Shivneri Fort is an ancient military fortification located near Junnar in Pune district in Maharashtra, India. It is the birthplace of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, the founder of the Maratha Empire. The fort is situated on a hilltop that overlooks the Junnar city. It is a triangular-shaped fort with seven well-defended gates. The fort is surrounded by steep cliffs on all sides, making it difficult to attack. There are many water tanks and wells within the fort complex, which provided a steady supply of water to the fort’s inhabitants.
FORT: SINDHUDURG
LOCATION: Malvan, Maharashtra
Sindhudurg Fort is an imposing maritime fortress located on an island in the Arabian Sea, just off the coast of Malvan in the Konkan region of Maharashtra, India. It was built by the legendary Maratha warrior-king, Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, in 1664-1667. The fort’s strategic location, formidable defenses, and historical significance make it a popular tourist destination and a symbol of Maratha valor.
FORT: SINHAGAD
LOCATION: Pune, Maharashtra
Sinhagad Fort, also known as Kondhana Fort, is an ancient hill fortress located approximately 30 kilometers southwest of Pune, India. It stands atop a 1,320-meter (4,330-foot) hill and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding Sahyadri Mountains. The fort has a rich history dating back to the 2nd century BCE and has witnessed numerous battles and power struggles. It is particularly renowned for the Battle of Sinhagad in 1670, where Tanaji Malusare, a brave Maratha warrior, valiantly captured the fort from the Mughal forces.
FORT: SUVARNADURG
LOCATION: Ratnagiri, Maharashtra
Suvarnadurg Fort, situated off the coast of Maharashtra, India, is a historic sea fortress with a fascinating past dating back to the 17th century. Built by Shivaji Maharaj, it was strategically located to control maritime trade and defend against potential invaders. The fort's name, which means "Golden Fort," reflects its economic significance during the Maratha rule. Today, Suvarnadurg stands as a notable historical site, attracting visitors interested in maritime history and admiring its architectural remains that highlight Maharashtra's glorious past as a maritime power.
FORT: TORNA
LOCATION: Pune, Maharashtra
Torna Fort, also known as Prachandagad, is a majestic hill fort located in Pune district, Maharashtra, India. Standing tall at an elevation of 1,403 meters (4,603 feet) above sea level, it is the highest hill fort in Pune district and a prominent landmark in the Sahyadri mountain range. Its strategic location and historical significance make it a popular destination for trekkers, history buffs, and nature enthusiasts alike.
FORT: VELLORE
LOCATION: TAMIL NADU
Vellore Fort, located in Tamil Nadu, India, is a 16th-century fort renowned for its grand granite walls and a wide moat. Built during the Vijayanagara Empire, it later came under the control of the Bijapur sultans, the Marathas, and the British. The fort also houses a Hindu temple, a mosque, and a church, showcasing religious harmony.
FORT: VIJAYDURG
LOCATION: Devgad, Maharashtra
Vijaydurg Fort, situated in Maharashtra, India, is a significant coastal fortress with a history dating back to the 12th century. Originally built by the Shilahara dynasty, it later came under the control of various rulers, including the Marathas and the British. Renowned for its strategic maritime location, Vijaydurg Fort served as a vital naval base and played a crucial role in protecting the region's coastline. Its robust architecture, massive walls, and historical importance make it an essential part of Maharashtra's cultural heritage and a testament to its military significance.
